The Constitution of the Republic of Kazakhstan

We, the people of Kazakhstan, united by a common historic fate, creating a state on the ancient Kazakh land, considering ourselves a peace-loving and civil society, dedicated to the ideals of freedom, equality and concord, wishing to take a worthy place in the world community, realizing our high responsibility before the present and future generations, proceeding from our sovereign right, accept this Constitution.
Section I. General Provisions Article 1. 1. The Republic of Kazakhstan proclaims itself a democratic,

secular, law-governed and social State whose highest values are the human (person), his life, rights and freedoms. 2. The fundamental principles of the activity of the Republic of Kazakhstan are public concord and political stability; economic development for the benefit of all the nation; Kazakhstani patriotism; resolution of the most important issues of the affairs of state by democratic methods including voting at a national referendum or in the Parliament.

Article 2.

1. 2.

The Republic of Kazakhstan is a unitary State with a presidential form of government. The sovereignty of the Republic of Kazakhstan extends over its entire territory. The State ensures integrity, inviolability and inalienability of its territory. The administrative-territorial division of the Republic of Kazakhstan location and the status of its capital are determined by law.

The names "Republic of Kazakhstan" and "Kazakhstan" have the same meaning.

Article 3. 1. 2. The people are the only source of State power. The people exercise power directly through a national referendum and free elections as well as delegate the execution of its power to State organs. Nobody can appropriate power in the Republic of Kazakhstan. Appropriation of power is persecuted by law. The right to act on behalf of the people and the State belongs to the President and Parliament of the Republic of Kazakhstan. The government and other State organs may act on behalf of the State only within the limits of their authority. The State power in the Republic of Kazakhstan is unitary and exercised on the basis of the Constitution and laws in accordance with the principle of its division into the legislative, executive and judicial branches and a system of checks and balances that governs their interaction.

Article 4. 1. The provisions of the Constitution, the laws corresponding to it, other regulatory legal acts as well as regulatory resolutions of the Supreme Court of the Republic of Kazakhstan shall be the functioning law in the Republic of Kazakhstan. The Constitution has the highest juridical force and direct effect on the entire territory of the Republic of Kazakhstan. International treaties ratified by the Republic of Kazakhstan have priority over its laws and are directly implemented except in cases when the application of an international treaty shall require the promulgation of a law. All laws [and] international treaties of which the Republic of Kazakhstan is a party are published. Official publication of 12

The Republic of Kazakhstan recognizes ideological and political pluralism. No ideology may be established as State ideology. The merging of public and State institutions, and the formation of party and trade union organizations in State organs is prohibited. Public associations are equal before the law. Illegal interference of the State in the affairs of public associations and of public associations in the affairs of the State, imposing the functions of State institutions on public associations, and financing of public associations by the State is prohibited. It is prohibited to form public associations pursuing the goals or actions directed toward a violent change of the constitutional system, violation of the integrity of the Republic of Kazakhstan, undermining the security of the State, inciting social, racial, national, religious, class and tribal enmity, as well as the formation of unauthorized paramilitary units. Activities of political parties and trade unions of other States as well as financing political parties and trade unions by foreign legal entities and citizens, foreign States and international organizations is not allowed in the Republic. Activities of foreign religious associations on the territory of the Republic as well as appointment of heads of religious associations in the Republic by foreign religious centers shall be carried out in coordination with the respective State institutions of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

Section II. Man and Citizen Article 10. 1. Citizenship of the Republic of Kazakhstan is acquired and terminated as prescribed by the law, is uniform and equal regardless of the grounds of its acquisition. Under no circumstances can a citizen of the Republic of Kazakhstan be deprived of citizenship or of the right to change his citizenship, and also he cannot be exiled from the territory of Kazakhstan. Foreign citizenship of a citizen of the Republic of Kazakhstan is not recognized.

Human rights and freedoms belong to everyone by virtue of birth, are recognized as absolute and inalienable, and define the contents and implementation of laws and other regulatory legal acts. Citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan have rights and bear responsibilities owing to their citizenship. Everyone has the right to confidentiality of personal deposits and savings, correspondence, telephone conversations, postal, telegraph and other messages. Article 23. 1. Citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan have the right to freedom of forming associations. The activities of public associations are regulated by law. Section III. The President Article 40. 1. The President of the Republic of Kazakhstan is the head of the State, its highest official determining the main directions of the domestic and foreign policy of the State and representing Kazakhstan within the country and in international relations. 23

2. 3.

2.

The President of the Republic of Kazakhstan [is the] symbol and guarantor of the unity of the nation and the State power, inviolability of the Constitution, [and the] rights and freedoms of the person and citizen. The President of the Republic of Kazakhstan safeguards the concerted functioning of all branches of State power and responsibility of the organs of power before the nation.

3.

Article 41. 1. The President of the Republic of Kazakhstan is elected in accordance with the constitutional law, by the citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan who have come of age, by universal, equal and direct suffrage under a secret ballot for a five-year term. A citizen of the Republic of Kazakhstan is eligible for the office of President of the Republic of Kazakhstan if he is not younger than thirty-five and not older than sixty-five, and has a perfect command of the State language and has lived in Kazakhstan not less than ten years. Regular elections of the President of the Republic of Kazakhstan are held on the first Sunday of December and may not coincide with the election of a new Parliament of the Republic. Extraordinary elections of the President of the Republic are held in cases and within terms established in paragraph 1 of Article 48 of this Constitution. Elections are deemed valid if more than 50 percent of the registered voters participate in the voting. The candidate who receives more than 50 percent of the votes of the constituents that took part in the election shall be deemed elected. If none of the candidates receive the above number of votes, a second round of elections shall be held between the two candidates who obtained the largest number of votes. The candidate who receives the larger number of votes by the voters who take part in the second round of elections shall be deemed elected.

Section IV. Parliament Article 49. 1. 2. Parliament is the highest representative organ of the Republic of Kazakhstan performing legislative functions. The term of Parliament is four years. The powers of the Parliament begin from the opening of its first session and terminate with the first session of a new convocation. The powers of Parliament may be prematurely terminated in cases and according to the procedure specified by the Constitution. The organization and activities of the Parliament, [and] the legal status of its deputies are determined by a constitutional law.

3.

4.

Article 50. 1. 2. Parliament consists of two Chambers acting on a permanent basis: the Senate and the Majilis. The Senate is composed of deputies elected in two persons from each oblast, major city and the capital of the Republic of Kazakhstan, at a joint session of the deputies of all representative bodies of the respective oblast, major city and the capital of the Republic. Seven deputies of the Senate shall be appointed by the President of the Republic for the term of Parliament. The Majilis consists of sixty members who are elected from one-mandate territorial electoral districts which are established in accordance with the administrative-territorial division of the Republic and which have approximately an equal number of voters. A deputy of Parliament can not be a member of both Chambers simultaneously.

Regular election of members of the Majilis takes place not later than two months prior to the expiration of the term of the Parliament. The election of members of the Senate is realized on the basis of indirect voting practiced secretly. One-half of [the] members of the Senate comes up for re-election every other year. At the same time regular election takes place not later than two months prior to the expiration of their term. Extraordinary election of members takes place within two months of a premature suspension of the members' term. A deputy of the Majilis can be a citizen of the Republic who has reached twenty-five years of age. The candidate who receives the larger number of votes of the voters who took part in the second round of elections shall be deemed elected. Decisions and resolutions of akims can be annulled respectively by the President, the Government of the Republic of Kazakhstan or a senior akim, as well as by a judicial process.

Section IX Concluding and Transitional Provisions

Article 90.

1.

The Constitution of the Republic of Kazakhstan adopted at a national referendum shall come into effect from the day of official publication of the results of the referendum. Simultaneously, the previously adopted Constitution of the Republic of Kazakhstan ceases its effect.

2.

The day of adoption of he Constitution at the national referendum shall be proclaimed a national holiday — the Constitution Day of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

Article 91.

1.

Amendments and additions to the Constitution of the Republic of Kazakhstan can be introduced only by a national referendum held by the decision of the President made on his own initiative, at the proposal of Parliament or the Government. However, the draft of amendments and additions to the Constitution shall not be submitted to a national referendum if the President decides to pass it to the consideration of Parliament. In this case, Parliament's decision shall be adopted according to the procedure established by this Constitution. The unitary form and territorial integrity of the Republic, [and] the forms of government cannot be changed.
