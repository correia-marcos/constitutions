Chapter I. Preliminary Provisions ART. 1. The Panamanian nation constitutes an independent State. Its form of Government is republican, democratic and unitary, and its name is the Republic of Panama. 2. Public authority extends to the whole of the territory of the Republic, to the atmospheric space, to its territorial waters, to its warships and to merchant ships navigating under the Panamanian flag, and to all other places jn which it appertains to the Republic of Panama to exercise rights of jurisdiction in accordance with international law. 3. The following are organs of public authority: the Legislative Power, the Executive Power and the Judicial Power, which shall exercise their respective functions separately and within limits but co-operating harmoniously in the fulfilment of the purposes of the State. The National Assembly constitutes the Legislative Power.

The President of the Republic and the Ministers of State constitute the Executive Power. The Supreme Court of Judicature and the other courts established by law constitute the Judicial Power. 4. The territory of the Republic is formed by- that lying between the Republics of Costa Rica and of Colombia, within the respective frontiers fixed or to be fixed in accordance with international law and public treaties, the adjacent islands in both oceans and all extents of territory which for any reason are or ought to be under Panamanian jurisdiction. Additional Clause. Limitations of jurisdiction provided for in public treaties concluded prior to the present Legislative Act amending the constitution are recognised. 5. The territory of the Republic is divided into provinces and the latter into districts. The law may create regions (comarcas) subject to special regimes and may set up other territorial divisions for specific purposes. The Provincial Councils may subdivide the districts into parishes (corregimientos) in accordance with administrative requirements. 6. For the creation or suppression of a province it will be necessary that the law suppressing or creating it be approved after debate on third reading by two-thirds of the number of deputies constituting the National Assembly. 7. The nation has the right of eminent domain over all the territory constituting it, including the territorial waters, soil and sub-soil, and over all property within it. 8. The flag, anthem and coat of arms of the Republic shall be adopted by law. 9. The capital of the Republic shall be the city of Panama. In case of war, earthquake or other calamity constituting a grave danger for the inhabitants of the capital, or of serious disturbance of the public peace, the Executive Power may transfer the capital temporarily to another point in the national territory. 10. Spanish is the official language of the Republic. It is a function of the State to watch over its purity, preservation and teaching throughout the country.

Panamanian nationals by birth are: (a) Persons born within the jurisdiction of the Republic, whatever be the nationality of their parents, provided that neither of the latter falls within the category of prohibited immigrants. (b) Persons born within the jurisdiction of the Republic, even though one of the parents may be in the category of prohibited immigrants, provided the other is a Panamanian by birth. This provision shall not apply when the parent who, besides being in the category of prohibited immigrants, belongs to the negro race whose original language is not Spanish. (c) Persons born outside the jurisdiction of the Republic of a father or mother Panamanian by birth, provided that one of them is not in the category of prohibited immigrants. 00 Persons born before November 3. 1903, within the territory which today forms the Republic of Panama. 13 (transitional). The President of the Republic may recognise the status of Panamanian national by birth as being enjoyed by children born within the jurisdiction of the Republic of a father or mother belonging to races falling within the category of prohibited immigrants, provided that they prove that they have belonged to homes established within the jurisdiction of the Republic during the whole of their minority or that part of it which has elapsed, and that their usual language is Spanish. This provision shall also apply when one of the parents falls within the category of prohibited immigrants and the other is not a Panamanian by birth. The President of the Republic may only exercise this faculty if the person concerned submits his application within the period of 3 months starting from the date on which the present amendment comes into force. 14. The following persons may be Panamanian by naturalisation provided that they do not fall within the category of prohibited immigrants: (1) Foreigners, single or married, who have resided within the jurisdiction of the Republic for more than 5 years: foreigners, married, who have resided for more than 3 years within the jurisdiction of the Republic and have had children born of their marriage within the Republic of Panama; and

(2) Immigrants who establish themselves in the country and devote themselves to the work of agriculture, stock-raising, breeding of birds and other similar or derivative industries and declare their desire to acquire Panamanian nationality. (3) Foreigners who took part in the independence movement of 1903. Additional Clause. The law shall regulate the details. 15. Persons included in the preceding article must apply for letters of naturalisation to the President of the Republic, who may refuse the same for reasons of public health, morality or security. Additional Clause. The rights to be enjoyed by those persons who obtain provisional letters of naturalisation shall be determined by law. 17. Foreigners who have acquired Panamanian nationality by naturalisation before this constitutional amendment came into force shall retain such capacity. 18.
